Flood Management: Protective Steps

Learn essential steps to protect yourself, your family, and your property from floods.

Before a Flood: Preparation is Key

  • Know Your Flood Risk: Check if your area is prone to flooding by visiting local government sites or consulting flood maps.
  • Create an Emergency Plan: Establish a family emergency plan that includes evacuation routes and meeting points.
  • Protect Your Home: Install sump pumps, elevate electrical appliances, and consider flood insurance.

During a Flood: Staying Safe

  • Follow Local Authorities' Guidance: Listen to the radio or official websites for updates and evacuation instructions.
  • Evacuate if Instructed: Use designated routes, and bring your emergency kit and documents.
  • Protect Yourself Indoors: Move to higher floors, shut off electricity, and avoid touching electrical equipment.

After a Flood: Recovery and Safety

  • Avoid Floodwater: Floodwater can contain harmful bacteria and debris. Avoid contact as much as possible.
  • Begin Cleanup Safely: Wear protective gear and dry areas exposed to water to prevent mold growth.
  • Document Damage for Insurance Claims: Take photos of damage and contact your insurance provider.
